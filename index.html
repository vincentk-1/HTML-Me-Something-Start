The American Presidency

A quick rundown of the basics

The elections is around the corner again. To win, a candidate must receive 270 Electoral votes, which is half of the 538 total. These votes equal to the number of the senators and house of representatives from all the states. The District of Colombia gets three even though it has no representatives in congress.

Qualifications

  • A natural-born citizen (can be born abroad of parents who are American Citizens).
  • At least 35 years of Age.
  • A resident of the United States for at least 14 years (but not necessarily the 14 years just preceding the election).

Benefits

  • The White house
  • A salary of $400,000 per year
  • Expense account of $50,000 per year
  • Travel expenses of $100,000 per year (tax free)
  • Pension, on retirement, equal to that of a cabinet member ($221,400)
  • Staff support and Secret Service protection on leaving the presidency
  • A White House staff of 400-500 persons
  • A place in the country: Camp David
  • A personal airplane: Air Force One
  • A fine chef.

The Powers of the President

  • Serve as commander in chief of the armed forces
  • Commission officers of the armed forces
  • Grant reprieves and pardons for federal offences (except impeachment)
  • Convene Congress in special sessions
  • Receive ambassadors
  • Take care that laws be faithfully executed
  • Wield the “executive power”
  • Appoint officials to offices
  • Approve legislation passed by Congress
  • With Senate's confirmation: Make treaties, appoint ambassadors, judges and high officials

Unique History of the Presidency.

Did you know that at one point in history we have had both the president and the vice president who were not elected to their offices? In 1973, vice president Spiro Agnew resigned, and President Nixon nominated Gerald Ford as his vice president who was confirmed by congress and sworn in. ⇛ Gerald Ford nomination

The following year, Nixon resigned the presidency and Ford became the president. Ford nominated Nelson Rockefeller who was confirmed by both houses of congress again. ⇛ Rockefeller nomination
